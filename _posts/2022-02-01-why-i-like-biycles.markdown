This is my first note and also a love declaration for bicycles, I guess. 

I remember the first time I rode a bicycle without training wheels as if it was yesterday. The moment is imprinted in my mind and I can exactly see the yellow, red and blue tones of my first BMX in front of me. Although I grew up in Rome, which is not exactly a bike-friendly city, biycles have played important part in my life and they still do today.

When I was a student in the Netherlands, living on a tight budget, my bike allowed me to move freely around the city. Riding a bike was a form of entertainment, a way to stay fit and a necessity. My heart was crushed when my city bike got stolen. I quickly moved on, however, and bought a second-hand one and lived frugually for a while.

In Berlin, where I moved to make my first professional steps after graduating, bicyles continued playing an important role. Despite employers offering public transportation benefits, I've always preferred moving around the city by biycle: be it scorching hot, rainy, snowy or cold, I prefer biycles than the suffocating feeling of a crowded subway compartment.

Call me crazy if you'd like, but I think that there is nothing that comes as close to the feeling of freedom as riding a bicyle. I look forward to many more years of bike riding and fun tours with friends or solo.
